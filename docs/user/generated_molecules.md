# Generated Molecules

## Safety Notice

molecule-ranker is for research use only. It provides no medical advice, no
clinical claims, no lab protocols, no synthesis instructions, and no dosing.
Generated molecules require validation before they can be treated as anything
more than computational hypotheses.

## What Generated Molecules Mean

Generated molecules are computational hypotheses. In V1.1 they are produced
through a deterministic AgentGraph scientific design workflow with source-backed
seed/scaffold traceability, target-conditioned design objectives, oracle scoring,
medicinal chemistry critique, uncertainty/diversity estimates,
experiment-readiness triage, and active-learning design metadata. The generated
molecule itself has no direct evidence unless an exact imported result is later
linked from a user-supplied file.

## How To Interpret Them

Generated scores and V1.1 report-card fields help prioritize review. They do not
prove binding, activity, safety, novelty, synthesizability, or feasibility. Do
not describe generated molecules as confirmed or clinically meaningful.

## Required Checks

Before review, confirm that generated entries are labeled with origin
`generated`, include limitations and a V1.1 report card, do not cite fabricated
sources, do not include synthesis or protocol content, and do not reuse evidence
from a different molecule.

## Practical Use

Use generated molecules to form review questions and computational follow-up
ideas. Keep them separate from existing candidate evidence, assay results,
active learning outcomes, integrations, Codex summaries, and dashboard claims.
