# Literature Evidence

## Safety Notice

molecule-ranker is for research use only. It provides no medical advice, no
clinical claims, no lab protocols, no synthesis instructions, and no dosing.
Generated molecules require validation before they can be treated as anything
more than computational hypotheses.

## What Literature Evidence Means

Literature evidence records source-backed claims and provenance. It should use
real source identifiers from retrieval systems and should never fabricate paper
IDs, titles, citations, assay results, or clinical claims.

## How To Read Evidence

Inspect claim direction, source record IDs, query provenance, limitations, and
contradictory findings. Literature mentions are not proof of candidate utility.
They are signals for ranking scores and review.

## Common Checks

- Confirm citations came from retrieval, not Codex text.
- Confirm generated molecules do not inherit literature evidence from seeds.
- Confirm report limitations explain uncertainty.
- Confirm dashboards separate evidence from review decisions.

## Relationship To Other Outputs

Literature evidence informs ranking, generated molecules, developability
review, assay results interpretation, active learning, integrations, Codex
summaries, and dashboard views, but it does not make clinical claims.
