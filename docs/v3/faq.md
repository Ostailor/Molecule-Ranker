# V3 FAQ

## Common V3 Boundaries

- No medical advice.
- No clinical validation.
- No lab protocols.
- No synthesis instructions.
- No dosing.
- Generated hypotheses require independent validation and human review.
- Codex output is not scientific truth.

## Is V3 A Clinical System?

No. V3 is for internal research planning. Its validation and certification
artifacts are software and autonomy validation artifacts, not clinical
validation.

## Does A Ranked Candidate Mean The Candidate Works?

No. Ranking organizes planning signals and source-backed evidence. It does not
prove binding, activity, safety, efficacy, manufacturability, or therapeutic
value.

## Are Generated Molecules Or Antibodies Real Evidence?

No. Generated outputs are computational hypotheses. They require human review
and independent validation.

## Can Codex Approve A Stage Gate?

No. Codex may prepare context, but human approval is required for stage gates,
external writes, generated-output advancement, campaign activation, policy
override, autonomy escalation, and other governed actions.

## Why Are External Writes Disabled?

External writes can affect systems outside the result bundle. V3 disables them
by default and requires human approval when they are enabled.

## What Does Certification Prove?

Certification proves that configured platform and workflow checks passed. It
does not prove scientific truth or clinical validity.

## What Should Humans Review First?

Start with certification findings, lineage, evidence summaries, generated
hypotheses, unresolved contradictions, high-risk model predictions, weak
structure conclusions, portfolio selection, and campaign finalization.
