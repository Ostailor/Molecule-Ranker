# Human Governance

V3 workflows are human-governed. Agents may plan, summarize, validate, and run
approved deterministic tools, but they cannot approve their own risky actions.

## Common V3 Boundaries

- No medical advice.
- No clinical validation.
- No lab protocols.
- No synthesis instructions.
- No dosing.
- Generated hypotheses require independent validation and human review.
- Codex output is not scientific truth.

## Human Approval Required

Human approval is required for:

- External write.
- Generated molecule advancement.
- Generated antibody advancement.
- Campaign activation.
- Stage gate approval.
- High-cost job.
- Destructive action.
- Policy override.
- Autonomy escalation.
- Tool package approval.
- Support bundle with logs or transcripts.
- `write_approved_live` workflow.

## Human Review Required

Human review is required for:

- Generated hypotheses.
- Generated antibodies.
- Critical developability or safety risks.
- Unresolved contradictions.
- High-risk model predictions.
- Weak structure or docking conclusions.
- Campaign plan finalization.
- Portfolio selection finalization.

## Codex Self-Approval Is Blocked

Codex can prepare approval requests, summarize context, and identify missing
information. Codex cannot approve external writes, generated-output advancement,
stage gates, campaign activation, or policy overrides.

## Governance Artifacts

V3 outputs include governance validation, approval summaries, review queues, and
the human governance matrix. These artifacts document what was approved, what is
pending, and what must not proceed without a human decision.
